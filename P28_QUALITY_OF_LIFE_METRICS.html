QUALITY_OF_LIFE_METRICS

In SURVIVAL, I sloppily attempted to communicate my (outdated) opinion that suicide is the only moral way for a person to die and that striving for immortality is the only moral way for a person to live. (As of now, that opinion is obsolete because my personal standards of morality have evolved).

In this page, I want to posit that a life worth living is one which is determined by its inhabitant to generate more pleasure than suffering (both as an “amplitude” metric and as a “frequency” metric).

Of course, minimally satisfying suffering-to-pleasure ratios for both metrics will vary from one person to another (since each person is a repository of unique experiences).

By “amplitude”, I mean intensity of the affect of an experience which is either predominantly suffering or else predominantly pleasure (while excluding half-and-half combinations as a subjective experiential category).

By “frequency”, I mean intensity the number of times a sufficiently intense experience which is either predominantly suffering or else predominantly pleasure occurs within a particular interval of time.
